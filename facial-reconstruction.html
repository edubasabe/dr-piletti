FACIAL RECONSTRUCTION

Dr. Piletti has been devoted to the reconstruction
of facial deformities caused by trauma, congenital
disease and skin cancer.

NASOLABIAL RECONSTRUCTION
(BASOCELULAR CARCINOMA)

The ideal choice for this lesion is the nasolabial flap, which is designed so that the cheek scar is hidden within the nasolabial crease.

The color and texture of the cheek tissue are similar to the lesion area, and the proximity facilitates easy transposition of the flap. In this particular case, you can see a mild bump, which is an elevated and bulging scar deformity due to a lymphatic and venous obstruction caused by the trapdoor effect. With time, it will improve and may even disappear.

PARRY ROMBERG SYNDROME
(PROGRASIVE HEMIFACIAL ATROPHY)

This is an infrequent neurocutaneous syndrome (acquired disorder) that is characterized by shrinkage and atrophy of the skin and soft tissues of half of the face. In rare cases, this can occur in both sides of the face. The pathogenesis of this syndrome is unknown, but it could be a variant of scleroderma.

Dr. Piletti has treated successfully these patients using fat transfer.
